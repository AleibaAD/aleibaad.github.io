About Me

I am a development economics professional specializing in quantitative analysis and data-driven policy work across climate, education, health, and gender issues.

Research

Climate Shocks, Repeated Exposure and Adaptation: Evidence from Ethiopia Distinction

University of Oxford Master's Dissertation

This research examined how repeated exposure to climate shocks influences adaptive behaviors among smallholder farmers in Ethiopia. Using panel data and advanced econometric techniques, I found evidence that repeated exposure to droughts increases the likelihood of adopting climate-resilient agricultural practices.

Gender Gaps in Agricultural Productivity: Evidence from Sub-Saharan Africa

Working Paper

An analysis of the factors contributing to gender gaps in agricultural productivity across multiple African countries, with policy recommendations for addressing these disparities.

Education Outcomes and Economic Shocks: A Longitudinal Study

Forthcoming

Research investigating how economic shocks affect educational attainment in low-income communities, with implications for social protection policies.
